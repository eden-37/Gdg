About flowers

flower, the characteristic reproductive structure of angiosperms. As popularly used, the term “flower” especially
applies when part or all of the reproductive structure is distinctive in color and form.Given that there over
352,000 species of flowering plants known to science, flowers have a seemingly endless array of colors, sizes,
forms, and anatomical arrangement. They range in size from minute blossoms to the giant blooms of Rafflesia and
the titan arum. In some plants, such as poppy, magnolia, tulip, and petunia, each flower is relatively large and
showy and is produced singly, while in other plants, such as aster, snapdragon, and lilac, the individual flowers
may be very small and are borne in a distinctive cluster known as an inflorescence. Regardless of their variety,
all flowers have a uniform function, the reproduction of the species through the production of seed.

    properties of flower
  1. Reproduction: Flowers facilitate sexual reproduction in angiosperms, producing pollen (male) and ovules (female) for seed development.
  2. Parts (Whorls): Typically arranged in four whorls: Calyx (sepals), Corolla (petals), Androecium (stamens), Gynoecium (pistil/carpels).
  3. Attraction: Petals attract pollinators with color, while nectaries provide sugary food, and strong scents (aromatic oils) draw insects, birds, and bats.
  4. Support: A stalk (pedicel) supports the flower, with the receptacle holding the parts.
  5. Symmetry: Flowers can exhibit radial (regular), bilateral (irregular), or no symmetry (asymmetric).
